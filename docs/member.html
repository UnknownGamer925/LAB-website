Our Standards for new members

Thank you for your interest in joining Lympstone Academy Brass. Whilst we are a training band, our
playing standards are good for a band of our nature. In order that you can take part fully in our playing,
we would like all those who join us to be able to read music prior to joining and, if you have not played a
brass instrument before, we would ask that you have some private tuition outside of the Band before
joining in. Our Band Members use a number of locally based music teachers and we'd be happy to
provide recommendations if needed.
We practise every Saturday from 10am to 12pm in the practice room on Chapel Road, except those
Saturdays when we have gigs. We expect members to attend as frequently as they can and notify a
committee member of their absances in advance. This is also expected for any Gigs aswell.

What do I need?

If you have your own instrument this would be an advantage, because, although we have some spare
instruments to lend out, they are not always available. If you do not have an instrument, please contact
us to see if we have one available for you.
When you join us, we will provide you with music and a folder to keep it in. Please bring the music to
practices. For gigs, a list of music will be sent out in advance and Band Members are expected to
organise their music accordingly. We provide cornet mutes which are kept with us at all times and
provided on-site so you do not take them home. Given the younger nature of some of our players, we
only use cup mutes. We do not use mutes for other instruments.

Ready to Join?

If you think you are ready to become a member of Lympstone Academy Brass, please read the Welcome
letter(a) available on this page for additional information. Then contact us to see if a position is available
(Please note there may be a delay before we get back to you). In the meantime, download and print out
forms b - e. These require you to read through and sign them and need to be handed in when you arrive for
your first practice.
